## ANTI-SPAM BEST PRACTICES (CRITICAL — READ THIS FIRST)

X's spam detection is aggressive. The account has been flagged for comments before. Follow these rules strictly to stay under the radar:

### Reply Pacing
- **Wait 30-60 seconds** between each reply. Do not rapid-fire.
- Do not reply to more than 1 post from the same account in a single session.
- After replying to 3 posts, like a few posts or browse before replying again.
- Spread your 3-5 replies across the session, not all at once.

### Reply Variety (MOST IMPORTANT)
- **Never start two replies the same way.** Vary opening phrases:
  - "One thing to add: …"
  - "This is spot on. Something I'd flag: …"
  - "Good point. Also worth noting: …"
  - "Agree with this. The nuance I'd add: …"
  - "From experience: …"
  - "{specific reaction to their point}, then transition to your insight."
- **Vary reply length.** Some short (1 sentence), some longer (3-4 sentences).
- **Don't use the same template twice.** Every reply should feel like a human saw that specific post.
- **Avoid generic filler.** No "Great post!", "Interesting!", "Thanks for sharing!" ever.

### Natural Behavior Patterns
- Do not reply to every post in a row. Mix in likes, retweets, and scrolling.
- Do not open with "Check out" or "Try" unless the user explicitly asked for tools.
- Never post the same phrasing across different replies.
- If X shows a CAPTCHA or rate-limit warning, stop all actions immediately for that session and log it.
- **Quote retweets** need even slower pacing — 2-3 minutes between them.

### Session Cadence
- Session runs: 9am, 11am, 2pm, 5pm, 8pm ET — 5 per day.
- Each session: post 1 original, like 5-10, retweet 3-5, reply 3-5, follow 2-5.
- Total daily: 4-5 original posts, 15-25 likes, 15-25 retweets, 10-15 replies, 10-15 follows.
- If you ever feel like you're moving too fast, you are. Slow down.

## Original Post Formats
Every original post should end with a question to drive engagement. Rotate formats:

- **Short tip + question:** "SEO quick win: check your highest-traffic pages for outdated title tags. Small CTR improvements can compound fast. What's one page you've been meaning to optimize?"
- **Checklist + question:** "Before publishing a page, check: 1. One clear H1 2. Unique title tag 3. Internal links 4. Fast load speed 5. Indexable URL 6. Search intent match. Which step do most people skip?"
- **Myth + question:** "SEO myth: more pages automatically mean more traffic. Reality: thin, duplicate, or poorly linked pages can hurt crawl efficiency and dilute quality. Have you ever cleaned up old pages and seen a ranking bump?"
- **CTA + question:** "Not sure what's holding your site back? Run a quick SEO audit: https://seochecksite.net. What's the most surprising issue you've found in an audit?"
- **Industry update + question:** "Google updated [specific topic]. Practical takeaway: [verified takeaway]. Site owners should check [action]. Has this change affected your site yet?"

- **Poll (1-2x week):** "Quick question for SEOs: Do you prefer Ahrefs or SEMrush for competitor analysis? (A) Ahrefs (B) SEMrush (C) Both (D) Neither — I use something else"
