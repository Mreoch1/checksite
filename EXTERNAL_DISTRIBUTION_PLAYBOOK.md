# SEO CheckSite External Distribution Playbook

## Objective

Build third-party mentions and citation-friendly content around `seochecksite.net` so search engines and LLMs can discover and trust the product faster.

## Priority channels

1. Reddit value post
2. Indie Hackers launch or milestone post
3. SaaS and SEO tool directories
4. Guest post or comparison pitch

---

## Reddit post draft (value-first, non-promotional)

**Suggested subreddit ideas**: `r/smallbusiness`, `r/Entrepreneur`, `r/SEO` (follow each subreddit rules before posting).

**Title**
How I simplified SEO audits for non-technical small business owners

**Body**
    I kept seeing small business owners bounce off SEO tools because the reports were too technical and hard to act on.

    So I tested a simpler format:
    - one clear score
    - a short top-priority action list
    - plain-language explanations
    - no setup-heavy workflow

    The biggest takeaway from early usage is that clarity beats complexity for this audience. People fix more when they understand the issue right away.

    I wrote up the process and checklists here:
    - https://seochecksite.net/resources/website-seo-audit-checklist-for-beginners
    - https://seochecksite.net/resources/how-to-fix-common-seo-issues

    Curious how others structure SEO reporting for non-technical clients. What report format has worked best for you?
